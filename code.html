OUR MISSION

The mission of the Integrated Circuit Design Club is to cultivate an environment of experiential learning where members are empowered to acquire and enhance the skills necessary for a successful career in the core Electronics and Communication domain. Through hands-on projects, industry interactions, and collaborative initiatives, the club strives to nurture technical competence and innovation among its members.

OUR VISION

The Integrated Circuit Design Club envisions becoming a vibrant center of excellence in both analog and digital circuit design, providing innovative solutions for various applications. By fostering strong collaboration between industry and academia, the club aims to address the evolving needs of core industries, thereby bridging the gap and promoting technological advancements in the field.

Experiential Learning Activity

🚀 Experiential Learning in Action: Open Book Test on Linear Integrated Circuits! 📖🔍

To enhance conceptual understanding and problem-solving skills, we recently conducted an open book test on Linear Integrated Circuits (LIC) for our fourth-semester students. Rather than testing memorization, this approach encouraged critical thinking, application-based learning, and real-world problem-solving. Four sets of questions were there. Students were made into four groups, each group 18 students. Each question set consists of 18 questions.

Topics: Differential Amplifier- CMOS design, Current Mirrors, Op-amp circuits- Linear, non-linear, application circuits.
This test was a preparation before their Test 1 exam which starts on 26th March 2025. Questions were set in such a way that students can solve it and get confidence and motivation to do numerical.

💡 Why an Open Book Test?
✔ Encourages students to focus on understanding concepts rather than just recalling facts.
✔ Promotes the ability to analyze and apply knowledge in practical scenarios.
✔ Develops research and referencing skills, crucial for engineering problem-solving.

The results? Engaged students, insightful discussions, and a deeper grasp of LIC concepts! This initiative is a step towards bridging theory with practical application in engineering education.

Kudos to all the students for their enthusiastic participation! 🎉

Course Co-ordinator: Dr. Remya Jayachandran, Assistant Professor, ECED, NIE Mysore
